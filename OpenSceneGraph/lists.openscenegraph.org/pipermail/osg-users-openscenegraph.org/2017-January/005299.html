Hi Chris,

Just my two cents:
As a beginner, when I started to use OSG, learning from the beginner guide and the net tutorials, I was quickly able to get a good rendering. 
However, due to my lack of knowledge in OpenGL, at some point I get stuck in m development. 

So I've decided to buy a book on OpenGL to learn it first then come back to OSG.

To summarize:
It's better to use OSG because a grand number of functionalities (and all the OpenGL boiler plate code) are already present, tested and used by others.
But I would suggest to first learn the basics of OpenGL.


I hope it helps.
